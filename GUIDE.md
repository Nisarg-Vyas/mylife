## What is MyLife?

MyLife is a personal productivity website made specifically for college students. It has four main sections:

| Tab | What it does |
|-----|-------------|
| 🎓 College | Track your semester, courses, exams, vacations, and a full calendar |
| 📊 Track | Log a daily habit or activity and see your patterns over time |
| 📔 Diary | Write daily journal entries, track milestones, and log expenses |
| ✨ Insights | See streaks, scores, mood trends, and spending stats at a glance |

Your data is saved to the cloud, so it's available on **any device, any browser** — phone, laptop, or computer.

## Getting Started

### Step 1 — Open the website
Go to the live link above. You'll land on the **login screen**.

### Step 2 — Create your account
- Click **"Create Account"**
- Enter your name, email, and a password (minimum 6 characters)
- Click the **Create Account** button
- You're logged in! ✓

> **Tip:** You can also click **"Continue with Google"** to sign in instantly using your Google account — no password needed.

### Step 3 — Set up your semester
Once you're logged in, you'll see the **College tab**. Click **"Start Setup"** and follow the 4-step wizard. This takes about 2 minutes and sets up your entire semester view.

## Tab 1 — 🎓 College

This is the **main page** you see when you log in. It is your college dashboard.

---

### 🏫 BTech Journey Banner

At the very top of the College tab, there is a **golden banner** showing your overall BTech progress. It displays:

- **Days Left** — exact number of days remaining until you graduate
- **Months Left** — same, in months
- **Years Left** — same, in years (shown as a decimal, e.g. 2.4 years)

> **To set this up:** Click the avatar icon (top right corner) → click **"⚙️ BTech Settings"** → enter your degree name, college name, start date (when you joined college), and expected end date (when you graduate) → click **Save**.

---

### 📊 Semester Stats

Below the banner, there are **4 stat cards** showing:

| Card | What it shows |
|------|--------------|
| **Days Left** | Days remaining until your semester ends |
| **Vacation Days** | Total number of vacation days in this semester |
| **Exam Days Left** | Days remaining until your exam period ends |
| **Sem Completed** | What % of the semester has passed |

### 🎓 Semester Setup (4-Step Wizard)

Click **"✏️ Edit Semester"** to open the setup wizard. It has 4 steps:

#### Step 1 — Semester Info
- Enter your **Semester Name** (e.g. "Semester 5" or "3rd Year Sem 1")
- Enter the **Start Date** — the first day of your semester
- Enter the **End Date** — the last day of your semester
- Click **Next →**

#### Step 2 — Courses
- Type a **course name** in the box (e.g. "Data Structures")
- Choose whether that course **Has Exam** or **No Exam** from the dropdown
- Click **Add** — the course appears in the list below
- Repeat for all your subjects
- Courses with no exam are noted separately so you don't forget them
- Click **Next →**

#### Step 3 — Exam Period & Papers
- Enter the **Exam Period Start** and **End** dates (the overall exam window)
- Then add **individual paper dates**:
  - Type the paper name (e.g. "Mathematics III")
  - Select the date of that paper
  - Click **Add**
  - Repeat for each paper
- Click **Next →**

#### Step 4 — Vacations & Holidays
- Type the **vacation name** (e.g. "Diwali Break", "Winter Vacation")
- Select the **From** and **To** dates
- Click **Add** — the vacation appears in the list
- Repeat for all your vacations
- Click **✓ Save Semester**

Everything is now saved and your dashboard updates automatically.

## Tab 2 — 📊 Track

This tab is for tracking **one specific habit or activity** every day — like exercise, studying, reading, or anything else you want to monitor. It also has a private feature for tracking something you may be doing with or without a stimulus (referred to privately as "it").

---

### Setting Your Tracking Activity

At the top of the Track tab:
1. Type what you want to track in the text box (e.g. "Exercise", "Reading", "Meditation")
2. Click **Set**
3. A blue tag appears confirming what you're tracking

---

### The Monthly Grid

A calendar grid shows the current month. Each day cell shows:
- The **date number**
- How many times you did the activity that day (e.g. `2×`)
- A 👁 icon if you did it "with it"

**Colour coding:**
- 🔴 Red number = done while watching/doing "it"
- 🟢 Green number = done clean (without "it")
- 🟡 Yellow = mixed

At the bottom of the grid, you see the **monthly summary:**
- **Month Total** — total count for the month
- **With "it"** — how many times it was with the stimulus
- **Without** — how many clean times

**Navigate months** using the ← and → buttons.

---

### Logging an Entry

To log what you did on a day:

1. **Click any day** in the grid — it auto-fills that date in the log form below
2. Or manually pick a **Date** in the log form
3. Enter the **Count** — how many times you did it that day
4. Select from the dropdown:
   - **"No — clean 💪"** — you did it without any stimulus
   - **"Yes — with 'it' 👁️"** — you did it while watching/using "it"
   - **"Mixed"** — some of both
5. Add an optional **Note** (e.g. "Felt great", "Was tired")
6. Click **💾 Save Entry**

A green toast notification confirms your entry is saved.

> **To edit a past entry:** Click that date in the grid, change the values, and click Save again. It overwrites the previous entry.

## Tab 3 — 📔 Diary

Your personal diary. Every day has its own page where you can track milestones, log expenses, and write a journal entry. The calendar shows your day's "score" at a glance.

---

### The Diary Calendar

At the top is a monthly calendar. Each day shows:
- The **date number**
- A **% score** (e.g. `75%`) — how well you did on your milestones that day
- A **mood emoji** (if you set one for that day)

**Score colours:**
- 🟢 Green = 70% or above (great day)
- 🟡 Yellow = 40–69% (okay day)
- 🔴 Red = below 40% (tough day)

Click any date to open that day's diary page.

---

### ⚙️ Setting Up Milestones (Do this first!)

Milestones are the goals you want to hit each day. They power the % score on the calendar.

Click **"⚙️ Edit Milestones"** (top right of the Diary tab) to open the editor.

**To add a milestone:**
1. Type the milestone name (e.g. "Exercise", "Study 2 hours", "No social media", "Water intake")
2. Choose the **type:**
   - **Digital (✓/✗)** — either you did it or you didn't (checkbox)
   - **Analogue (number)** — you enter a value (e.g. how many glasses of water, how many hours studied)
3. Choose whether it **counts for your score:**
   - **"Counts for score"** — this milestone affects your daily % score
   - **"Doesn't count"** — tracked but not included in the score (e.g. a note-type milestone)
4. Click **Add**

Repeat for as many milestones as you want. Click **Done** when finished.

> **To delete a milestone:** Click the ✕ button next to it in the editor.

---

### Opening a Day's Diary Entry

Click any date on the calendar. A diary page slides open below with these sections:

---

#### 😊 Mood
At the top right of the diary page header, there is an **emoji**. Click it to cycle through moods:
`😢 → 😟 → 😐 → 🙂 → 😊 → 😄`

Choose the emoji that best represents how you felt that day. It gets saved and shown on the calendar.

---

#### 📊 Day Score Bar
A progress bar showing your score for the day (0–100%), calculated from your scored milestones. It updates live as you check things off.

---

#### ✅ Milestones Section
All your milestones appear here.

- **Digital milestones** — show a **checkbox**. Tick it if you completed it.
- **Analogue milestones** — show a **number input**. Type the value (e.g. 8 for 8 glasses of water).

The score bar updates immediately as you check/fill milestones.

Each milestone shows a small badge:
- `☑ Digital` or `∿ Analogue`
- `· scored` if it counts toward your score

---

#### 💸 Expenses Section
Track your spending for the day.

1. Click **"+ Add"**
2. An expense row appears with two fields:
   - **Description** — what you spent on (e.g. "Lunch", "Auto rickshaw", "Books")
   - **Amount (₹)** — how much you spent
3. Add as many rows as needed
4. The **Total** is calculated automatically at the bottom
5. Click ✕ on any row to delete it

---

#### 🖊️ Journal Section
A large text box where you can write freely about your day:
- How you felt
- What happened
- Thoughts and reflections
- Anything you want to remember

There is no word limit. Write as much or as little as you want.

---

#### Saving the Entry
Click **"💾 Save Entry"** to save everything — milestones, expenses, journal, and mood — all in one click.

Click **"Close"** to collapse the diary page without losing data (milestones auto-save as you check them).

## Tips & FAQ

**Q: Can I use this on my phone?**
Yes! The website works on mobile browsers. Open it in Chrome or Safari on your phone, and it will adapt to the screen size.

**Q: I forgot to write my diary for yesterday. Can I still fill it in?**
Yes. Just go to the Diary tab, click the **← arrow** to go to the previous month if needed, and click yesterday's date. Fill everything in and save.

**Q: Can I edit a tracking entry I already saved?**
Yes. Click that day in the tracking grid — it pre-fills the form with your saved values. Change whatever you want and click Save again.

**Q: What if two courses have the same exam date?**
Add both papers with the same date. Both will appear as chips on that calendar day.

**Q: I added a wrong vacation. How do I delete it?**
Go to **Edit Semester → Step 4**. Each vacation has an ✕ button — click it to remove it. Then click **Save Semester**.

**Q: Do my milestones apply to all diary days?**
Yes. Whatever milestones you have set up appear on every diary day. If you add a new milestone, it will also appear on past days (but those past days will show it as unchecked/zero).

**Q: What is a Tithi?**
Tithi is the Hindu lunar calendar date. The calendar shows it in small text on each day in the format `S-Ekadashi` (Shukla Ekadashi) or `K-Ashtami` (Krishna Ashtami). `Purnima` = full moon. `Amavasya` = new moon.

**Q: What does "it" mean in the Tracker?**
"It" is intentionally vague — it can mean anything you want to track in relation to a stimulus or trigger. For example, some people use this to track whether they exercised with or without watching a screen. You define what "it" means for you.

**Q: My data isn't showing after I logged in on a new device. What do I do?**
Wait a few seconds — data loads from the cloud after login. If it still doesn't show, try refreshing the page.
